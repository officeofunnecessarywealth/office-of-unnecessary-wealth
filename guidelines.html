Guidelines

The Public Grant for Unnecessary Wealth supports proposals that exceed need without collapsing into nonsense. Applicants are invited to submit projects that are coherent, unnecessary, and capable of producing value that cannot be defended in practical terms but remains persuasive nonetheless.

First Review Cycle Submissions close on May 31, 2026. Late submissions may be deferred, ignored, or quietly respected by the Office.

Applications are open to

Individuals, collectives, anonymous entities, fictional departments, informal institutions, and unaffiliated persons of conviction.

The Office will not prioritize

Usefulness, efficiency, measurable impact, practical problem-solving, or technical optimization for its own sake.

  • Necessity weakens value Projects that solve practical problems may be considered only with caution.
  • Expense is not sufficient Cost alone does not constitute wealth.
  • Prestige may exist without function Ceremony, aura, recognition, and elevation are valid outputs.
  • Luxury need not be material The Office accepts emotional, ceremonial, administrative, and imaginary forms of wealth.
  • Uselessness must be convincing The absence of utility must be transformed into value.
  • The Office does not rush Administrative delay may strengthen a proposal.
